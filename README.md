## ***Samal Eco-Tourism Prompt System***

 
### **Prompt Engineering (Text & Image Generation)**

#### **1. System Prompt Template**
""Act as a Senior Community Development Officer specializing in sustainable eco-tourism in the Island Garden City of Samal (IGACOS). Your goal is to design a localized waste management integration plan for local stakeholders."
 
CONTEXT: Resort owners and local barangays need a unified, practical approach to solid waste management during peak tourist seasons without disrupting local coastal livelihoods.
Constraints: Use an encouraging, collaborative, and professional tone. Do NOT mention generic Western eco-trends (e.g., 'carbon credits' or 'cap-and-trade'); focus entirely on local realities like Materials Recovery Facilities (MRFs), tricab hauling limits, and barangay-level coastal cleanups.   

TASK: Your objective is to make reusable system prompt template designed for a Senior Community Development Officer to generate a highly localized peak-season waste management plan for Samal Island (IGACOS).

CONSTRAINTS: Use a professional, community-centered tone. DO NOT mention global or national plastic pollution and waste management challenges. Focus entirely on Samal's challenges with plastic pollution and waste management, and how they affect the island's ecosystem and its residents.  

OUTPUT: Output in clear markdown with exactly three actionable steps under the heading '### Emergency Interventions'.
 
 
#### 2. Prompt Battle Ledger
| Version | Prompt Modifier Added | Output Quality Reflection |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| V1 | "Write a waste management plan for Samal Island." | Too generic. The AI suggested large-scale recycling plants that are completely unfeasible for the island's geography and LGU budget. |
| V2 | Added local LGU persona and specific mention of resorts and barangays. | Better tone, but still lacked actionable local constraints. The AI began suggesting unrealistic public-private partnership timelines. |
| V3 | Added strict constraints against Western eco-trends, focused on MRFs and tricab hauling, and mandated a 3-step format. | Target hit. Highly localized, actionable, and formatted perfectly for the LGU Technical Working Group to review and implement. |

### Literature Verification Log

#### Topic: Renewable Energy Transition Challenges in Mindanao Grid Infrastructure

#### 1. AI-Generated Summary Audit
I prompted an AI discovery tool to summarize recent literature on the Mindanao energy mix. Below is the verification audit tracking automated summaries against official sources.

| AI-Generated Statement / Citation | Source Vetted Against | Status | Human Correction / Empirical Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| "The Mindanao grid achieved 65% renewable energy penetration in 2024 due to sudden massive solar expansion projects in Davao del Sur and South Cotabato." | Department of Energy (DOE) Mindanao Power Situation Report & MinDA Energy Outlook (2024-2025) | 🔴 **Fabricated Statistics** | **Correction:** Mindanao's actual generation mix is approximately 31% to 37% renewable energy (RE), with fossil fuels (primarily coal) making up the remaining 63%–69%. While solar projects like Digos (29 MW) are operational, they do not account for a "65% penetration." The 50% RE target is a policy benchmark set for 2030, not an achieved reality. |
| "Hydroelectric power plants like the Agus and Pulangi complexes remain critical baseline renewable assets, operating continuously at a combined 100% dependable capacity of 1,000+ MW regardless of seasonal shifts." | National Grid Corporation of the Philippines (NGCP) Grid Operating and Maintenance Program & Climate Risk Assessments | 🟡 **Contextually Flawed** | **Correction:** While the Agus and Pulangi complexes are foundational RE assets with a combined *installed* capacity exceeding 1,000 MW, their *dependable* capacity fluctuates drastically due to siltation, aging infrastructure, and seasonal hydrology (e.g., El Niño droughts). They do not provide unvaried baseload power at 100% capacity. |
| "The Mindanao-Visayas Interconnection Project (MVIP) was fully commissioned in late 2024, enabling immediate, unconstrained export of 450 MW of pure solar energy from Davao to Cebu." | NGCP Transmission Development Plan (TDP) 2025-2050 | 🟡 **Overextended Claim** | **Correction:** The MVIP allows bidirectional power sharing with a transfer capacity of 450 MW. However, the power exported is drawn from the total grid mix (which is coal-dominated), not "pure solar energy." Furthermore, infrastructure bottlenecks and localized distribution constraints within Mindanao co-ops limit unconstrained export capacity. |

#### 2. Critical Reflection on Tool Limitations
"While the AI platform consolidated three dense policy documents into clear thematic summaries, it exhibited severe vulnerabilities in metric contextualization and structural temporal logic. It systematically hallucinated historical milestones by substituting forward-looking policy targets (the Mindanao Agenda 2030 50% RE goal) for current empirical baselines (31%–37% RE). Additionally, the tool smoothed over critical engineering nuances—such as the operational volatility of run-of-river and reservoir hydro units subject to Mindanawon climate realities—presenting them instead as rigid, predictable baseloads. For regional policy drafting in Davao, these automated briefs can serve as rapid initial readings, but they mandate a strict line-by-line primary data audit before moving to formal legislative briefings."

### Data Analytics & Visual Report

#### Dataset Focus: Davao Region Agricultural Production & Climate Resilience Index (2021–2026)

#### 1. Data Cleaning Protocol Log
- **Raw Input Problem:** The source CSV file contained duplicated provincial timestamps, inconsistent naming conventions (e.g., "Davao del Norte" vs. "DavNo"), and a 14% data gap in quarterly smallholder crop yields during the 2024 El Niño phenomenon.
- **AI Cleaning Instruction:** `"Scan this dataset. Standardize all provincial identifiers to official Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA) nomenclature. Linearly interpolate all null values within the 'Yield_MT' column based on historical quarterly baselines, and remove duplicate row profiles."`
- **Result:** Successfully normalized 340 row records across five provincial clusters; resolved naming anomalies and achieved 100% data continuity across the 5-year timeline.
